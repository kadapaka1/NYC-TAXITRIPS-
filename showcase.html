Key Findings
  • Rush hour adds ~4.2 minutes on average (weekday AM/PM windows).
  • Airport-bound trips (JFK/LGA/EWR) are ~2.3× longer than intra-Manhattan rides.
  • Distance explains most variance, but temporal + borough interactions tighten the error bars.

Feature Engineering Showcase

The model learns “how NYC moves” through compact features: geodesic distance, neighborhood context, and time-of-day rhythm. Feature names are shown as code-style badges.

haversine_distance_km

Haversine Distance

Great-circle distance between pickup and dropoff taxi-zone centroids — a robust proxy for trip length.

d = 2R · asin( √( sin²((φ₂-φ₁)/2) + cos(φ₁)cos(φ₂)sin²((λ₂-λ₁)/2) ) )
is_rush_am / is_rush_pm

Rush Hour Flag

Binary flags for congestion windows that spike duration independent of distance.

AM: weekday & 7–9
PM: weekday & 17–19
PU_borough / DO_borough

Borough Encoding

Pickup + dropoff borough one-hot encodings capture cross-borough travel penalties.

onehot(PU_borough) ⊕ onehot(DO_borough)
hour_sin / hour_cos

Cyclical time encoding

Hour-of-day and day-of-week encoded on a circle to represent periodicity without discontinuities.

hour_sin = sin(2π·h/24)
hour_cos = cos(2π·h/24)
is_airport_trip

Airport indicator

Detect trips near JFK/LGA/EWR by radius threshold; airports introduce predictable long-haul behavior.

is_airport = minDistTo({JFK,LGA,EWR}) < r
passengers_x_distance

Interaction term

Small but consistent: group trips tend to be longer and less direct, especially outside Manhattan.

passengers_x_distance = passenger_count · haversine_km
